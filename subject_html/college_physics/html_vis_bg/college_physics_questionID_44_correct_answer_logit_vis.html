The problem involves determining the minimum thickness of an oil film on glass that causes strong reflection of blue light (480 nm) when viewed normally. The oil has an index of refraction \( n = 1.2 \), and the glass has \( n = 1.6 \). When light reflects off a medium with a higher refractive index, it undergoes a phase shift of \( \lambda/2 \). Here, light reflects off both air (\( n = 1 \)) to oil (\( n = 1.2 \)) and oil (\( n = 1.2 \)) to glass (\( n = 1.6 \)). Both reflections cause a phase shift of \( \lambda/2 \), resulting in a total phase shift of \( \lambda \) (or \( 2\pi \) radians). For constructive interference, the path difference between the two reflected waves must equal an integer multiple of the wavelength \( \lambda \). The path difference is \( 2nt \), where \( t \) is the thickness of the oil film. Thus, the condition for constructive interference is: \[ 2nt = m\lambda \] Here, \( m \) is an integer. To find the minimum thickness (\( m = 1 \)): \[ t = \frac{m\lambda}{2n} \] Substituting \( \lambda = 480 \, \text{nm} \) and \( n = 1.2 \): \[ t = \frac{480}{2 \times 1.2} = \frac{480}{2.4} = 200 \, \text{nm} \] Thus, the minimum thickness of the oil film is **200 nm**.
